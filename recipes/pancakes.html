Pancakes

Back to recipes overview

Description

Pancakes are a flat cake, often thin and round, prepared from a starch-based batter that may contain eggs, milk, and butter and cooked on a hot surface such as a griddle or frying pan, often with oil or butter.

Another variant is to make a thick pancake, which are fluffier and thicker than the traditional thin pancakes. These are often referred to as "American-style" pancakes and are typically served stacked high with butter and syrup.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • Vegetable oil, for cooking

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another large bowl, whisk together milk, butter, and egg.
  3. Pour wet ingredients over dry ingredients and stir until moist.
  4. In a large nonstick skillet over medium heat, heat oil.
  5. For each pancake, spoon 2 to 3 tablespoons of batter onto skillet.
  6. Cook until surface of pancakes have some bubbles and a few have burst, 1 to 2 minutes.
  7. Flip carefully with a thin spatula, and cook until browned on the underside, 1 to 2 minutes more.
  8. Transfer to a baking sheet or platter; cover loosely with aluminum foil, and keep warm in oven.
  9. Continue with more oil and remaining batter. (You'll have 12 to 15 pancakes.)

Enjoy your pancakes!
